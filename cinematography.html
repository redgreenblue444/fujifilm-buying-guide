Codec Recommendations

  • H.265 (HEVC): Best compression efficiency, ideal for long-form content and storage-conscious projects. Supported by X-H2S, X-H2, X-T5, X-E5, X-S20, X-T50.
  • H.264: Universal compatibility, smaller file sizes. Good for quick turnaround projects and web delivery.
  • ProRes: Professional editing codec with minimal compression. Available on X-H2S and X-H2. Ideal for high-end productions and color grading workflows.
  • External RAW: Maximum quality and flexibility. X-H2S and X-H2 support external RAW recording via HDMI to Atomos or Blackmagic recorders.

Understanding F-Log vs F-Log2

F-Log

  • ~12-13 stops of dynamic range
  • Available on all X-Series cameras
  • Optimized for 10-bit recording
  • Good balance of DR and noise
  • Easier to grade than F-Log2

F-Log2

  • ~13+ stops of dynamic range
  • Available on X-H2S and X-H2 only
  • Optimized for 12-bit recording
  • Maximum dynamic range
  • Requires more careful exposure

Color Science & Film Simulations

Fujifilm's legendary film simulations are available even in video mode, providing instant color grading options. For maximum flexibility, shoot in F-Log/F-Log2 and apply film simulations in post-production using Fujifilm's official LUTs, or use them in-camera for quick turnaround projects.

Available Film Simulations: Provia, Velvia, Astia, Classic Chrome, Pro Neg. Hi, Pro Neg. Std, Eterna, Eterna Bleach Bypass, Classic Neg., Nostalgic Neg., and more (varies by camera model).

üîÑ Professional Video Workflow

Recommended Workflow for Professional Projects

1. Pre-Production

  • Choose camera based on resolution needs (8K for X-H2, 4K 120fps for X-H2S)
  • Select codec: ProRes for high-end, H.265 for efficiency
  • Plan for F-Log2 (X-H2S/X-H2) or F-Log (all cameras) recording
  • Test exposure and color workflow before shoot

2. Production

  • Shoot in F-Log/F-Log2 for maximum flexibility
  • Use external monitor for focus and exposure (false color, zebras)
  • Record to dual cards when available (X-H2S, X-H2, X-T5) for backup
  • Monitor temperature during long recordings (especially X-H2S with fan)
  • Use appropriate frame rates: 24p for cinematic, 60p for smooth motion

3. Post-Production

  • Import ProRes or H.265 files into your NLE (Premiere, DaVinci, Final Cut)
  • Apply Fujifilm's official F-Log to Rec.709 LUTs for color correction
  • For F-Log2, use the dedicated F-Log2 LUTs (available from Fujifilm)
  • Grade with the full dynamic range in mind - protect highlights and shadows
  • Export in your delivery format (4K, 1080p, etc.)

4. Color Grading Tips

  • F-Log2 offers more headroom - expose to the right (ETTR) for best results
  • Use waveform monitors and histograms during shooting
  • Fujifilm's color science is excellent - don't over-grade
  • Test LUTs before applying to entire project
  • Consider using film simulations as starting points for creative looks
